1 Irrecoverable losses:
  • Losses coming from the impossibility of distributing electricity to the final users due to the disruption of one or more assets of the distribution infrastructure;
  • Losses coming from the impossibility of performing metering activities and consequently charging the corresponding price to the final users.
2 Integrity loss of assets:
Assets partially or totally destroyed following a disruption.
3 Contractual penalties:
Applied for the interruption of electricity distribution to hospitals and other essential services:
  • Over 2 days (€ 1M)
  • Over 10 days (€ 5M)
  • Over 30 days (€ 20M)
1 Power outages:
Power outages with effects on:
  • Residential households
  • Commercial companies
  • Industrial sites
  • Transport infrastructure
  • Essential services
2 Delay in emergency response to accidents:
Delay in emergency response to accidents due to:
  • A disruption to monitoring and control systems
  • The inaccessibility of sites or unavailability of tools
Starting from the information about the types of losses, players will be able to evaluate the financial impact over time that Eldy would suffer following a disruption.
Players will have to fill the table calculating financial and societal impacts coming from the interruption of each activity for a certain number of days. For each activity, the severity of the impact over time can be evaluated in absent, low, medium or high.
Finally, the obtained results will be used to assess the Maximum Tolerable Period of Disruption (MTPD) and the Recovery Time Objective (RTO).


For each activity, the severity of the financial and societal impacts over time can be evaluated considering the following tables.
Severity of economic impacts.
0 No Impact Economic loss = € 0 No Economic loss
1 Low € 0 < Economic loss <= € 10M Economic Impact that can be absorbed but it requires an effort from management to absorb it
2 Medium € 10M < Economic loss <= € 40M Economic Impact that can be absorbed but it requires an extraordinary involvement of management
3 High Economic loss > € 40M Economic impact that requires a change of strategy

Severity of societal impacts.
0 No Impact No impacts on society
1 Low Impacts on society don’t affect the safety of citizens and the continuity of essential services
2 Medium Impacts on society are serious and require extraordinary measures to be managed
3 High Impacts on society are no more sustainable: the safety of citizens is compromised, essential services are disrupted and business activities are stopped
